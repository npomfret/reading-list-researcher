Summary

Recogard, a prominent curator of open-source trading tools on Twitter, announced the release of a comprehensive dataset covering 1.1 billion trading records from Polymarket prediction markets across 268,000+ markets. The dataset, totaling 107GB, was compiled and released for free by five graduate students from Shanghai universities (affiliated with Shanghai Innovation Institute, Westlake University, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Harbin Institute of Technology, and Fudan University). This represents the largest publicly available dataset of prediction market data, exceeding previous public datasets which contained only 36GB or less.

The tweet highlights the practical significance of this dataset for traders and researchers: it enables detailed analysis of Polymarket's actual market behavior and price movements over time. Users can analyze patterns within market categories to discover repeatable trading strategies based on historical data. For example, the dataset might reveal that economic markets tend to be less volatile than other categories and often have clear winners identified early, patterns that could form the basis of profitable trading strategies. Beyond individual trading, the dataset serves academic research purposes for studying prediction market dynamics, collective intelligence, and behavioral finance.

Alongside the raw dataset, the GitHub repository provides a complete toolkit for working with Polymarket data. This includes tools to fetch fresh data continuously via Polymarket's API and blockchain interfaces, process and clean the raw data, and convert it into analysis-ready formats (parquet files). The infrastructure supports both historical analysis of the complete dataset and real-time continuous monitoring of new trades. The dataset is available both on GitHub and through Hugging Face, with comprehensive documentation and MIT licensing for commercial and research use.

The announcement is significant because it democratizes access to prediction market data that was previously limited to paid third-party providers or proprietary traders. Polymarket is the world's largest prediction market platform (valued at $20+ billion as of 2026) and is increasingly being integrated into institutional trading workflows—Goldman Sachs now uses prediction market data in its oil market analysis, and the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) has created trading tools to consume Polymarket probability assessments. Having the underlying data freely available enables broader participation in market research and strategy development.

Key Takeaways

  • The dataset contains 1.1 billion trading records across 268,706 Polymarket markets from inception to present (before 2026), totaling 107GB in size—the largest freely available prediction market dataset.
  • Five analysis-ready dataset formats are provided: orderfilled (raw blockchain events, 293.3M records), trades (processed with market metadata, 293.3M records), markets (market metadata), quant (unified YES perspective, 170.3M records), and users (split maker/taker behavior, 340.6M records).
  • The toolkit enables continuous real-time data fetching with automatic block fetching (batch mode for gaps, real-time mode every 2 seconds when current), error recovery, and graceful shutdown—designed for 24/7 operation.
  • Researchers and traders can use the data to identify repeatable patterns (e.g., economic markets being less volatile with clear winners early) and develop quantitative trading strategies based on historical market behavior.
  • The dataset includes low-level blockchain details (block numbers, contract names, specific fee information) not available in third-party data sources, enabling deeper technical analysis.
  • Polymarket is increasingly integrated into institutional finance: Goldman Sachs uses prediction market data for oil market analysis, and ICE created a data feed for professional traders to consume Polymarket probabilities.
  • The MIT-licensed open-source approach contrasts sharply with paid data providers; comparable datasets from institutional sources or premium APIs cost significant fees.
  • The toolkit includes Python APIs, CLI commands, and shell scripts for batch processing, making the data accessible to researchers without deep blockchain expertise.
  • Use cases span market research (studying price discovery), behavioral studies (analyzing trader decision-making), academic research (collective intelligence and forecasting accuracy), and algorithmic trading strategy development.
  • The data is available through multiple channels: GitHub (complete source code and documentation), Hugging Face (simplified dataset download), and includes comprehensive examples for calculating market statistics, tracking price evolution, and analyzing user behavior.
