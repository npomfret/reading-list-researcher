Summary

Sarvesh Shrivastava, a TEDx speaker and #1 ranked SEO expert (by Favikon), announces he has repurposed Claude Cowork — Anthropic's autonomous AI agent — as his "Chief of SEO" and is publicly sharing his 8-part prompt system for local SEO audits and optimization. The post documents a complete framework for local service businesses (plumbers, HVAC, lawyers, etc.) to audit their entire Google presence, identify competitive gaps, and execute ranked improvements—work that traditionally took 30+ hours per month per client.

The system consists of eight sequential prompts designed to be run inside Claude Cowork, each automating a specific SEO function: Google Business Profile (GBP) category audits, attribute analysis, competitor review teardowns, review response templates, GBP post calendars, services section optimization, GBP description refinement, and photo upload planning. What distinguishes this approach from generic ChatGPT prompts is the requirement to pre-load business context—company details, competitor GBP URLs, existing categories—so Claude operates from real data rather than making generic recommendations.

Shrivastava emphasizes that execution order matters: weeks 1-2 focus on fixing foundational issues (categories and attributes) for quick ranking wins, weeks 3-4 optimize content and review systems, and week 5+ requires consistent human execution. He stresses that Claude Cowork performs the research and analysis (pulling competitor data from Google Maps, extracting attributes, analyzing reviews) but humans retain decision-making authority on strategy. The post includes detailed prompts, the reasoning behind each prompt, and specific examples of how each audit uncovers actionable competitive intelligence. Shrivastava claims this system, executed consistently over 90 days, can outrank established competitors and provides links to his consulting firm for businesses wanting professional execution of the framework.

Key Takeaways

  • Claude Cowork (not a chatbot but an autonomous agent) can open Chrome, visit Google Maps and competitor GBP listings, extract structured data, and generate prioritized spreadsheets—automating what used to take SEO agencies hours per week for each client.
  • The 8-part system prioritizes foundational GBP fixes first: correcting primary/secondary categories can immediately increase map pack visibility for high-intent searches, and adding missing attributes builds baseline credibility required in competitive markets.
  • Review velocity (reviews per month relative to competitors) is more important than star rating; the system calculates exactly how many reviews a business needs monthly to overtake competitors and extracts service keywords and location mentions from review text to identify SEO opportunities.
  • Pre-loading business data (company name, address, GBP URL, competitors, target keywords in files or folders) separates a tool from a system—Claude's output becomes business-specific rather than generic, reducing iteration needed.
  • GBP posts strategy emphasizes neighborhood-specific content (e.g., 'just completed kitchen remodel in [neighborhood]') which builds location authority; consistent weekly uploads signal to Google that the business is active, as most competitors post irregularly or not at all.
  • The sequential 4-week execution plan (foundation → optimization → reviews → content) is designed to show ranking improvements within days for category fixes, with compounding gains from consistent weekly execution reaching full impact at 90 days.
  • Photo consistency (3-5 new photos weekly) beats volume; before-and-after shots, team photos on job sites, and trucks in specific neighborhoods build trust and location relevance more effectively than generic office photos uploaded in batches.
  • GBP descriptions (750 characters) are underused keyword real estate; testing three variations (keyword-focused vs. conversion-focused vs. balanced) and measuring impressions and call conversions treats it as an optimization asset rather than a static element.
  • Review response templates with natural service and location keyword mentions turn 10 monthly reviews into 120+ pieces of keyword-rich content per year embedded directly on the GBP, improving both ranking signals and conversion rates.
  • The system requires human judgment for strategy decisions—deciding which keywords drive actual revenue, understanding local market nuances, and interpreting competitive patterns that data alone cannot reveal; Claude automates the research bottleneck, not strategy.

His presence predates the Claude Cowork hype—he was already a recognized SEO educator before pivoting to AI-assisted SEO in 2026. **Broader Context and Reactions:** Claude Cowork launched in January 2026 as Anthropic's autonomous agent feature (available on Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans). Since its launch, there's been a surge of SEO practitioners publishing Claude Cowork workflows. Key patterns in the ecosystem: (1) Ruben Dominguez published "Claude Can Now Do SEO Like a $10,000/Month Agency" with 15 copy-paste prompts reaching similar conclusions; (2) Reddit communities (r/SaaS, r/DigitalMarketing) report successful full SEO buildouts in 3-4 hours using Claude; (3) Multiple YouTubers and Substack writers have published Claude Cowork SEO guides, suggesting this is a trend, not isolated case. **Credibility Caveats:** While Shrivastava's background is verifiable, the claims about 90-day ranking dominance are anecdotal and not independently validated. Reddit discussions surface legitimate concerns: (1) "Garbage in, garbage out"—Claude's output depends entirely on prompt quality and data fed to it; (2) Google's March 2026 Core Update changed how it evaluates AI-mode content, creating potential outdating of prompt systems; (3) Human oversight required for critical decisions (choosing which keywords to target, interpreting competitive patterns, deciding brand positioning); (4) The system assumes a local business owner has time to execute consistently for 90 days, which many don't. Some seasoned SEO practitioners caution that this replaces junior analyst work but not strategy. **Why This Matters:** This post is significant because it exemplifies how AI agents (not just LLMs) are being deployed for business-critical tasks. It shifts the narrative from "AI writes content" to "AI executes workflows." The impact is democratizing: a solo entrepreneur can now do competitive research that used to require hiring analysts or agencies. However, it also raises questions about market disruption (agencies relying on junior SEO analysts face commoditization) and whether 2026 will see rapid consolidation in the SEO services market. **Important Limitation:** The post doesn't address: (1) How Claude Cowork's browser automation interacts with Google's ToS (terms of service); (2) Risk of IP blocking or rate limiting when Claude visits competitor GBP pages at scale; (3) Whether the prompts remain effective as Google updates its interface; (4) Local variations in GBP features (some countries/regions have different attributes and fields). These are execution risks the original post doesn't surface.
