Summary

Yinghao Xu, an Assistant Professor at HKUST and leading researcher in 3D computer vision, announced the open-source release of LingBot-Map, a novel 3D foundation model developed by Robbyant (Ant Group's embodied AI division) after one year of collaborative research. The model represents a significant breakthrough in real-time streaming 3D reconstruction, departing from traditional offline batch-processing approaches to enable continuous, online spatial understanding from standard RGB video feeds.

LingBot-Map is fundamentally different from its predecessors (DA3 and VGGT) in that it is a purely autoregressive feed-forward model rather than a bidirectional or iterative approach. It achieves approximately 20 FPS inference speed on 518×378 resolution video, maintaining stable performance even on sequences exceeding 10,000 frames—a critical capability for real-time applications in robotics, AR, and autonomous systems. The model's architecture is built on a Geometric Context Transformer (GCT) that unifies three key mechanisms: anchor context for coordinate grounding, pose-reference windows for dense geometric cues, and trajectory memory for long-range drift correction—carefully designed to keep streaming state compact while retaining rich spatial information.

The performance gains are substantial and validated across major benchmarks. On the challenging Oxford Spires dataset (known for large scale and difficult lighting), LingBot-Map achieves an Absolute Trajectory Error (ATE) of just 6.42 meters, representing a 2.8x improvement over the previous best streaming method. On the ETH3D benchmark for 3D reconstruction quality, it delivers an F1 score of 98.98, surpassing the next-best method by over 21 percentage points. Remarkably, LingBot-Map not only outperforms existing streaming methods but also surpasses several offline optimization-based approaches, demonstrating that real-time inference need not compromise on precision.

The release is strategic and significant within the broader context of embodied AI development. Robbyant positions this as a critical infrastructure layer complementing its existing stack—LingBot-Depth (for depth sensing), LingBot-VLA (a vision-language-action model for robot control), and LingBot-World (a real-time world model). By open-sourcing the code, pretrained models, and comprehensive demos under Apache 2.0 license through GitHub and Hugging Face, Robbyant deliberately lowers the barrier to entry for researchers and developers building spatial-aware AI systems. The inclusion of efficient inference optimizations (paged KV cache attention, FlashInfer support) and deployment options (CPU fallback, memory-efficient modes) reflects practical consideration of diverse hardware constraints faced by embodied AI applications.

  • Achieves approximately 20 FPS inference on 518×378 resolution while maintaining stability across video sequences exceeding 10,000 frames—meeting the real-time requirement threshold for robotics and AR applications.
  • The Geometric Context Transformer architecture unifies coordinate grounding, dense geometric cues, and long-range drift correction through three specialized attention mechanisms (anchor context, pose-reference window, trajectory memory).
